# Contributing

HTX Super League is currently in a documentation-first planning stage.

## Before making a change

1. Identify the business objective and the document that owns the decision.
2. Check the [founder decision register](docs/00-governance/founder-decisions.md).
3. Separate confirmed decisions from recommendations and assumptions.
4. Do not introduce application code until the product build gate is approved.

## Branch and pull request conventions

- Use short branches such as `docs/competition-rules` or `product/registration-scope`.
- Keep one coherent decision or deliverable per pull request.
- Explain what changed, why it changed, which decision authorized it, and what validation was performed.
- Request review from the business owner and the technical owner when a change affects both.
- Never commit credentials, participant exports, payment data, waiver signatures, medical details, or production secrets.

## Document status

Every decision-sensitive document should use one of these states:

- **Draft:** proposed and open for review;
- **Approved:** accepted by the authorized founders;
- **Superseded:** replaced by a newer document or decision;
- **Archived:** retained for history but no longer active.

Legal templates, waivers, policies, and insurance descriptions remain drafts until reviewed by qualified counsel and the applicable insurer or sanctioning body.

## Decision records

Use the [decision record template](docs/templates/decision-record-template.md) for material choices involving competition format, ownership, money, risk, customer commitments, data, or architecture.
