# Sponsorship and Partnership Strategy

**Status:** Draft  
**Decision owner:** Brand and commercial lead

## Commercial objective

Build a small number of credible, measurable relationships that improve the participant experience and reduce pilot cash exposure. Sponsor inventory must be deliverable even if media reach begins modestly.

## Partner categories

### Revenue partners

- Houston-area banks/credit unions and responsible financial services;
- healthcare, sports medicine, physical therapy, dental, and wellness providers;
- automotive dealers and mobility services;
- restaurants, food, beverage, and hospitality;
- telecommunications and technology;
- soccer retail, apparel, footwear, and equipment;
- real estate, home services, construction, and local professional services;
- employers recruiting in Houston;
- culturally relevant consumer brands.

### Operating partners

- venue and field operators;
- referee assignors and associations;
- medical and athletic training providers;
- photographers, videographers, and production teams;
- equipment and apparel providers;
- hospitality and awards vendors;
- community organizations and soccer clubs.

### Distribution and community partners

- soccer creators and local media;
- universities and alumni groups;
- cultural associations;
- community nonprofits;
- supporter and pickup groups;
- Houston soccer retailers and training programs.

## Category safeguards

Founders must approve prohibited and restricted categories. Exclude or subject to enhanced review:

- tobacco/nicotine;
- illegal or intoxicating products inconsistent with venue rules;
- weapons;
- predatory lending or misleading financial products;
- unverified supplements or medical claims;
- adult content;
- political campaigns;
- gambling, fantasy, or wagering products;
- businesses with discriminatory or materially unsafe practices.

Alcohol relationships, if considered for an adult-only competition, require legal, insurance, venue, age-control, placement, and brand review. Never connect alcohol to player performance or field access.

## Sponsor inventory

Sell only inventory that operations and product can track.

| Inventory | Pilot availability | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Presenting designation | One | Contract term, approved naming use, impressions, proof archive |
| Website and standings placement | Limited | Page views, clicks, placement dates |
| Schedule/results graphic placement | Limited, template-controlled | Posts/assets delivered and reach |
| Matchday signage | Venue-approved | Installation dates and photo proof |
| Content series | One sponsor per series/category | Published items, views, engagement |
| Awards naming/presentation | Selected awards | Event proof, content, attendee reach |
| Captain/player offer | Approved useful offer | Redemptions or tracked code |
| Sampling/activation | Venue- and product-approved | Attendance, units, leads with consent |
| Community access fund | Available | Players/teams supported and impact report |
| Photography/highlights | If production is contracted | Assets delivered and reach |

Do not sell control over rules, team acceptance, officiating, discipline, standings, editorial conclusions, private participant data, or player eligibility.

## Proposed package architecture

Final pricing follows audience validation and costed fulfillment.

### Founding Presenting Partner

One season-level relationship with category exclusivity where justified, lead approved placement, matchday presence, selected content/awards, hospitality, and a season report.

### Competition Partners

Limited partners tied to useful experiences such as performance/medical, content, awards, equipment, or venue. Each receives defined placements and deliverables.

### Community Partners

Smaller cash or in-kind relationships tied to captain offers, access support, activations, or community distribution. In-kind value is recorded separately from cash.

## Pilot commercial targets

- Base financial model assumes approximately **$8,000 cash sponsorship**; this is a target, not committed revenue.
- Seek 1 presenting/founding partner and 2–4 focused supporting partners rather than many low-value logos.
- Secure at least 70% of required sponsor cash before the league's non-refundable operating commitments reach the sponsor-dependent amount.
- Limit sponsor fulfillment cost and staff burden in the budget.

## Sales process

1. Qualify category fit, decision maker, objective, geography, budget, timing, and brand risk.
2. Share the venture story, validated audience, pilot scope, venue, calendar, and honest reach assumptions.
3. Build a package from available inventory and sponsor objectives.
4. Price cash and in-kind separately; cost fulfillment.
5. Approve category, conflicts, claims, creative, data use, and operational feasibility.
6. Contract payment schedule, deliverables, approvals, cancellation, makegoods, morality/brand safety, IP, data, and reporting.
7. Enter every deliverable in the fulfillment tracker before announcing.
8. Provide proof and season report; discuss renewal after pilot evaluation.

## Partner proposal content

- HTX Super League and TRIMNDS ownership story;
- specific pilot audience and competition product;
- market research and qualified team pipeline, clearly labeled;
- venue/calendar and match count;
- brand, website, matchday, media, and community inventory;
- category exclusivity boundaries;
- activation concept and fulfillment responsibilities;
- measurement plan;
- cash price, payment schedule, in-kind treatment, and taxes/fees;
- safety, inclusion, data/privacy, and brand-approval standards.

## Fulfillment tracker fields

- agreement and partner owner;
- category and exclusivity;
- deliverable, quantity, channel, due date, and dependency;
- required asset/approval and status;
- internal cost;
- delivery evidence link;
- performance measure;
- issue/makegood;
- invoiced, collected, and outstanding cash;
- renewal decision and feedback.

## Partnership measures

- cash contracted and collected;
- in-kind fair value and actual cash cost avoided;
- fulfillment completion and timeliness;
- sponsor reach, clicks, leads, redemptions, and activation attendance as applicable;
- participant feedback and complaints;
- internal fulfillment cost;
- partner satisfaction and renewal intent;
- venue and community referrals;
- incidents, conflicts, or unsupported claims.
