# Venture Charter

**Status:** Draft  
**Decision owner:** Marcus, Nesta, and David  
**Review date:** Before concept-validation spending is approved

## Purpose

HTX Super League exists to make organized Houston soccer feel dependable, competitive, accessible, and worth following. It will begin with a focused 9v9 competition and may expand only after the operating model is proven.

## Brand and ownership architecture

- **Customer-facing brand:** HTX Super League.
- **Venture owner and governor:** TRIMNDS.
- **Public endorsement:** “A TRIMNDS Venture” may appear on the About page, legal footer, partner materials, and corporate communications without displacing the HTX Super League identity.
- **IP ownership:** The name, marks, domains, content, competition formats, data structures, documents, and software should be assigned to the operating entity or licensed to it under a founder-approved structure.
- **Recommended legal structure:** Evaluate a dedicated HTX Super League operating entity owned by TRIMNDS to ring-fence league operations and liabilities. A Texas attorney and CPA must advise on the final structure, tax treatment, assumed-name filings, contracts, and IP assignments.

## Strategic scope

### Initial scope

- one Houston-area competition;
- one 9v9 adult division;
- team-led registration with individual player onboarding;
- fixtures, results, standings, basic statistics, discipline, payments, communication, and sponsor delivery;
- public website and lean administration tools;
- consistent matchday operations and media coverage.

### Deferred scope

- youth competition;
- multiple cities;
- multiple simultaneous formats;
- native mobile applications;
- live streaming for every match;
- advanced scouting or performance analytics;
- transfer markets, player compensation, or semi-professional status;
- custom software for capabilities that can be safely validated manually first.

## Governance

The founders must assign these accountabilities before external commitments are signed:

| Accountability | Required owner | Decision rights |
|---|---|---|
| Venture lead / commissioner | One founder or appointed operator | Day-to-day delivery within budget and policy |
| Competition | Named owner | Format, rules, scheduling, results, discipline administration |
| Finance and legal coordination | Named owner | Budget control, contracts, insurance, refunds, financial reporting |
| Commercial and brand | Named owner | Brand, marketing, sponsorship, media, community relationships |
| Product and technology | Named owner | Product scope, data, security, vendors, releases |

Reserved founder decisions should require at least two-founder approval, with conflicts disclosed and recorded. Reserved matters include:

- equity, debt, guarantees, and ownership changes;
- annual and pilot budgets;
- contracts above an agreed threshold or longer than one season;
- new locations, age groups, or competition formats;
- material changes to pricing, refunds, participant safety, or data use;
- use of TRIMNDS or HTX Super League IP by another party;
- appointment or removal of the venture lead;
- litigation, regulatory response, or material incident communication.

The founders must choose whether reserved matters use majority approval or unanimous approval and define a deadlock procedure.

## Success definition for the pilot

The pilot succeeds when HTX Super League demonstrates all of the following:

- the minimum number of paid teams is reached before the commitment deadline;
- every scheduled match is delivered or resolved under a published weather/cancellation policy;
- no material safety, legal, financial, or data-control failure occurs;
- results and standings are accurate and timely;
- captain and player satisfaction meet the approved target;
- team intent to return meets the approved target;
- the season stays within the approved cash-loss or margin envelope;
- sponsors and venue partners receive the contracted value;
- founders have evidence to decide whether to repeat, improve, expand, or stop.

## Stage gates

| Gate | Evidence required | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Validate | Interviews, competitor scan, demand list, venue options, preliminary budget | Venture lead + founders |
| Commit | Venue terms, insurance/sanctioning path, approved rules outline, legal work plan, approved budget | Founders |
| Sell | Brand foundation, offer, price, refund terms, sponsor inventory, registration process | Venture + finance/legal owners |
| Build | Approved MVP scope, architecture, data/privacy rules, product owner, delivery budget | Founders + product owner |
| Launch | Paid-team threshold, signed waivers, roster compliance, officials, medical plan, field readiness, tested systems | Commissioner |
| Continue | Pilot scorecard, financial close, incident review, retention evidence | Founders |

## Non-negotiable principles

- Safety and fair treatment take priority over keeping a match or schedule alive.
- Rules and commercial terms are published before participants commit.
- No founder, team, sponsor, or high-profile player receives undisclosed competitive preference.
- Discipline decisions are documented, time-bound, and appealable under the published process.
- Participant data is not sold.
- Growth follows repeatability; it does not precede it.
