# Rules, Policies, Waivers, and Risk Framework

**Status:** Planning framework; not legal advice or approved policy  
**Decision owner:** Finance/legal lead and commissioner  
**Required reviewers:** Qualified Texas counsel, insurance professional, venue, and sanctioning body where applicable

## Purpose

This document identifies the legal, safety, conduct, and operational controls required before HTX Super League accepts participants or runs matches. It does not replace legal advice, insurance terms, medical protocols, or governing-body requirements.

## Legal and entity workstream

Before contracting or taking payment:

- confirm the operating entity and its relationship to TRIMNDS;
- complete assumed-name/entity filings as advised;
- document IP ownership or licenses for brand, content, software, and competition assets;
- obtain tax, accounting, contractor, and sales-tax advice;
- approve contract-signing authority and spending thresholds;
- complete brand and trademark clearance;
- publish legal entity, contact, and policy information accurately;
- review privacy, marketing, messaging, photography, and biometric/identity verification practices.

The recommended structure is a dedicated HTX Super League operating entity owned by TRIMNDS, subject to legal and tax advice. Operational independence in the market does not remove the need for formal ownership, governance, contracts, and intercompany records.

## Sanctioning and insurance decision

Obtain written proposals for:

1. affiliation/sanctioning through the appropriate adult soccer structure, including TSSAS/USASA;
2. independent operation with commercial general liability, participant accident/medical, abuse/molestation where relevant, directors and officers, workers' compensation or occupational coverage, cyber, property/equipment, hired/non-owned auto, event cancellation, and umbrella/excess coverage as advised.

Compare:

- covered entities, participants, volunteers, contractors, events, ancillary activities, and venues;
- occurrence and aggregate limits;
- participant accident benefits;
- exclusions, deductibles, reporting deadlines, and defense terms;
- additional-insured certificates and contractual requirements;
- referee, coach, and player registration obligations;
- rules, discipline, suspension, appeal, and tournament requirements;
- cost per league, team, player, and event;
- effect of unsanctioned games, media activity, alcohol, prizes, vendors, and travel.

TSSAS identifies affiliation benefits including liability and medical insurance, an appeals system, referee pathways, consistent rules, and suspension visibility. USASA describes coverage for adult soccer premises and operations. HTX must review the actual current documents rather than relying on summaries: [TSSAS affiliation information](https://app.teampass.com/Texas_State_Soccer_Association_South/) and [USASA liability information](https://usadultsoccer.com/liability-insurance/).

## Safety minimums

- Documented venue-specific emergency action plan.
- Confirmed emergency address, vehicle access, evacuation route, and nearest appropriate care.
- Accessible AED and trained responder coverage as advised.
- First-aid supplies and incident forms.
- Medical/safety coverage matching venue, insurer, and risk assessment requirements.
- Participant emergency contact collected securely and available only to authorized staff.
- Field, goal anchoring, surface, lighting, boundary, equipment, and weather inspection before play.
- Immediate removal from play when a concussion or serious injury is suspected; return only under the approved medical protocol.
- Designated weather monitor who is not occupied as a referee or coach.
- Safe shelter identified before lightning risk.
- Heat and hydration modification rules based on a medically reviewed method appropriate to Houston conditions.
- No schedule or commercial pressure overrides the safety decision authority.

The National Weather Service recommends assigning a dedicated weather monitor for outdoor sports, and the CDC advises moving strenuous activity away from the hottest parts of the day and responding immediately to heat-illness symptoms. Sources: [NWS lightning safety](https://www.weather.gov/safety/lightning-sports), [CDC heat and athletes](https://www.cdc.gov/heat-health/risk-factors/heat-and-athletes.html), and [U.S. Soccer Recognize to Recover](https://www.ussoccer.com/about/recognize-to-recover).

## Incident classification and response

| Severity | Example | Initial response |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Life-threatening injury, violence, weapon, missing minor, major data/payment breach | Call emergency services where appropriate; stop activity; preserve safety/evidence; notify executive and insurer/counsel immediately |
| High | Hospital transport, assault allegation, referee threat, match abandonment, significant privacy exposure | Secure people; suspend involved access where justified; document; notify designated leaders and external parties per policy |
| Moderate | Injury requiring non-emergency follow-up, serious misconduct, field failure, material payment dispute | Apply protocol; record; assign owner and deadline; evaluate trend |
| Routine | Minor first aid, ordinary complaint, correctable score or service error | Resolve through standard workflow and record for quality review |

Incident records must be factual, time-stamped, access-restricted, and retained under an approved schedule. Do not investigate serious criminal, medical, abuse, or cyber matters beyond organizational competence; preserve information and engage the proper professionals and authorities.

## Risk acceptance

Every high or critical residual risk must have a named owner, treatment, due date, and founder acceptance before launch. Insurance transfers some financial risk; it does not replace prevention, compliance, or safe operations.

## Legal launch gate

Registration may open only after the operating entity, signing authority, participant terms, privacy notice, payment/refund terms, waiver process, competition rules, insurance/sanctioning path, venue contract, safety framework, and required customer contacts are approved and operational.
