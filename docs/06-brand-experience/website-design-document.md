## Purpose

This document defines the public HTX Super League website so brand and website work can proceed while league administration, legal structure, venue, sanctioning, rules, pricing, and the authenticated platform are still being resolved.

The first website should establish the brand, explain the competition, validate demand, collect qualified interest, support partnership conversations, and later become the public source of truth for the live season. It should not pretend that unresolved details are confirmed, accept participation payments before the appropriate gates are approved, or attempt to replace the future administrative platform.

## Product separation

HTX Super League has two related digital surfaces.

| Surface | Purpose | Initial scope |
|---|---|---|
| Public website | Brand, discovery, validation, competition information, public schedules/results, media, sponsors | Design and build can begin now |
| Authenticated platform | Team applications, player registration, waivers, payments, rosters, referee workflows, discipline, administration | Designed at workflow level; implementation follows the build gate |

They should eventually share a visual system and source data, but the public website must be able to launch independently with controlled forms and approved content.

## Website objectives

### Business objectives

- Establish HTX Super League as a credible independent competition brand owned through TRIMNDS.
- Generate qualified captain and team interest for the pilot.
- Support venue, referee, sponsor, media, and community partnership discussions.
- Explain the proposed 9v9 product without making unsupported claims.
- Build an owned audience before registration opens.
- Become the official source for public competition information once matches begin.

### User objectives

- Understand what HTX Super League is within seconds.
- Determine whether the competition is relevant to the visitor's role and level.
- See which details are confirmed, proposed, or not yet announced.
- Register interest without creating a full participant account.
- Find accurate dates, venue information, rules, schedules, results, and contacts when available.
- Trust that the league will be organized, fair, safe, and responsive.

### Primary conversion goals

1. **Captain or team interest submitted**
2. **Partner inquiry submitted**
3. **League updates subscription completed**
4. **Team application started**, only after registration opens

General traffic, social follows, and page views are supporting measures rather than primary evidence of demand.

## Brand role and positioning

HTX Super League should feel like a contemporary competition and sports-media brand, not a generic recreational-league template and not an imitation of a European professional club.

### Desired impression

- serious but accessible;
- locally rooted but not built from Houston tourism clichés;
- visually energetic but operationally precise;
- competitive without aggression;
- editorial and cinematic without overstating the scale of the pilot;
- modern enough to grow into statistics, fixtures, and media products.

### Positioning statement

For Houston teams and players who want serious competition without semi-professional complexity, HTX Super League delivers organized 9v9 competition with dependable operations, transparent rules, useful digital tools, and a matchday experience worth following.

### Proposed public-facing promise

**Every match should feel organized, competitive, and worth showing up for.**

This is a proposed brand promise, not the required hero headline.

### Language restrictions

- Do not use “Houston's first,” “largest,” “best,” “number one,” “official,” “professional league,” or “elite” without an approved and supportable definition.
- Do not call a proposed date, venue, price, format, or benefit confirmed until its decision owner approves it.
- Do not use emojis anywhere in the official website or interface.
- Do not use decorative numbered labels such as “01, 02, 03” to structure ordinary sections.
- Do not rely on excessive football slang, hostility, or claims about domination.
- Use “soccer” for market clarity; “the game,” “competition,” and “football culture” may appear naturally in editorial copy.

## Page design briefs

## Home

### Purpose

Create immediate understanding, establish the visual identity, communicate the competition promise, and direct visitors to the correct action.

### Recommended page sequence

#### Season status bar

A restrained line above or below navigation showing one current truth:

- Founding season interest now open
- Team applications now open
- Registration waitlist
- Opening match date
- Next featured fixture during the season

The status bar must be content-managed and removable.

#### Hero

Required content:

- short category statement;
- high-impact headline;
- one-sentence explanation;
- primary and secondary calls to action;
- one dominant image or a purposeful image sequence;
- a compact competition-status label.

Proposed pre-launch copy direction:

> Houston 9v9, built with purpose.

Supporting direction:

> A new competition for teams that expect organized matchdays, clear standards, and a season worth following.

Primary action: **Register Team Interest**  
Secondary action: **Explore the Competition**

Copy is provisional until the target segment and offer are approved.

#### Competition snapshot

Use compact factual blocks for approved or clearly labeled proposed information:

- 9v9 format;
- Houston area;
- eight-team pilot;
- adult 18+ proposed segment;
- Spring 2027 proposed window;
- league plus playoffs.

Do not display a provisional fact without a label such as “Planning baseline” or “Proposed.” Remove the label when the decision is approved.

#### Why HTX Super League

Four experience pillars:

- Reliable competition
- Clear standards
- Matchday presentation
- Digital clarity

Each pillar receives one concise sentence and one visual proof point. Avoid long generic benefit paragraphs.

#### For captains

Show how the league reduces captain burden:

- clear fees and deadlines;
- roster visibility;
- one official schedule;
- responsive support;
- transparent rules and discipline.

Action: **Register Team Interest** or **View Team Information**.

#### The matchday

A cinematic image-led section showing the intended atmosphere: arrival, warm-up, officials, competition, spectators, results, and recognition. Before HTX has original imagery, use licensed imagery carefully and do not imply it depicts an existing HTX event.

#### Competition information

A structured preview of format, level, season timing, and what is included. Link to the Competition page for detail.

#### Updates and media

Display the latest three meaningful stories or announcements. Before the league begins, suitable content includes founder notes, venue/format explanations, captain interviews, rules philosophy, and partnership announcements.

#### Partner invitation

A concise commercial section explaining the opportunity to help build a new Houston competition. Do not publish inflated audience numbers or sponsor logos without permission.

Action: **Explore Partnerships**.

#### Final conversion

Use a strong closing statement and the current primary action. Repeat essential expectation-setting such as “Interest does not guarantee team acceptance” when applicable.

## Visual direction

## Creative territory

The visual system should combine the energy of small-sided soccer with the discipline of a well-run competition. The reference point is an independent sports publication and competition system rather than a generic sports-registration vendor.

### Core visual characteristics

- large, compressed headlines;
- strong grid and alignment;
- bold monochrome or duotone photography;
- one controlled high-energy accent color;
- generous negative space around important statements;
- compact data typography for fixtures, scores, and standings;
- visible rules, borders, and dividers that imply structure;
- asymmetry used deliberately, never at the expense of scanning.

### Avoid

- generic shield-and-laurel decoration on every surface;
- fake metallic textures, flames, gaming effects, or excessive glow;
- a green grass background as the default sports cue;
- overuse of gradients;
- skyline, NASA, cowboy, state-outline, highway-sign, or other Houston clichés;
- stock photos of professional stadium crowds that imply a scale HTX has not reached;
- visual imitation of the English Premier League, UEFA, MLS, or a specific club;
- decorative numbering of content sections;
- autoplay hero video with sound;
- continuous marquees that reduce readability.

Houston identity should come through the name, teams, people, venues, stories, and community—not through tourism iconography.

## Digital color system

The implemented semantic tokens and contrast rules are maintained in [Digital Design System](digital-design-system.md) and `packages/design-system`. Components must use semantic roles so a future palette revision does not require page-level redesign.

Recommended initial territory:

| Token role | Provisional value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#0B0F14` | Primary dark background and text |
| Chalk | `#F4F1E8` | Warm light background |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | High-contrast text and clean surfaces |
| Volt | `#C8FF3D` | Primary action and competition accent |
| Steel | `#7C8793` | Large secondary display details only |
| Muted | `#626C76` | Normal secondary text on Chalk |
| Signal | `#F04F3D` | Warnings, urgent changes, disciplinary/error states |

Rules:

- Never place Volt text, icons, rules, or the mark on White or Chalk. Volt-on-White and Volt-on-Chalk are prohibited combinations.
- Use Ink on Volt for filled controls and status labels; use Volt on Ink only for short accent or display moments.
- Use Muted rather than Steel for normal secondary text on Chalk.
- Keep the primary and secondary marks monochrome. The retired Volt-eye treatment must not return in digital applications.
- Signal is semantic and should not compete with the primary action color.
- Check every foreground/background pair against WCAG 2.2 AA.
- Team colors may appear inside controlled team contexts but must not redefine global interface meaning.
- Sponsor colors appear only in approved assets or defined partner placements.

## Typography

### Recommended pairing

- **Display and competition headlines:** Barlow Condensed, weights 600–800
- **Body and interface:** Manrope, weights 400–700
- **Scores and tables:** Manrope with tabular numerals enabled

The pairing is provisional until the logo and identity are approved, but it gives designers and developers a usable starting point.

### Type scale direction

| Role | Desktop direction | Mobile direction |
|---|---:|---:|
| Display hero | 88–120 px | 52–68 px |
| Page title | 64–80 px | 42–52 px |
| Section title | 42–56 px | 32–40 px |
| Card title | 22–30 px | 20–26 px |
| Body large | 18–22 px | 17–20 px |
| Body | 16–18 px | 16–18 px |
| Label/data | 12–14 px | 12–14 px |

Use responsive fluid sizing within controlled minimum and maximum values. Do not compress body copy below 16 px to fit layouts.

### Typography rules

- Use sentence case for navigation, buttons, and ordinary headings.
- Uppercase may be used for short competition labels, statuses, and data headers.
- Avoid long paragraphs in condensed display type.
- Align numeric data consistently and use tabular figures in scores and standings.
- Ensure text remains real HTML rather than being embedded in images.

## Photography and video

### Photography style

- player- and team-led rather than facility-led;
- close, low-angle, and inside the action;
- visible effort, preparation, concentration, and community;
- nighttime lights, texture, movement, and controlled motion blur;
- varied wide, medium, portrait, detail, and environment frames;
- respectful representation across Houston's soccer communities;
- referees and operations included as part of the competition story.

### Pre-launch image policy

Licensed stock photography may be used to establish visual direction before HTX events exist, but it must:

- fit the actual 9v9/adult amateur context;
- avoid recognizable professional club kits and protected event branding;
- not imply that pictured people or venues participate in HTX;
- have documented web, social, and advertising rights;
- be replaceable through a defined original-content plan.

### Original content plan

Before registration campaign launch, commission a controlled brand shoot using founders, invited players, referees, and an approved field. Capture:

- hero landscape images;
- vertical mobile/social crops;
- captain/player portraits;
- boots, ball, lines, goals, lights, and match preparation;
- referee and operational detail;
- empty-field and venue utility imagery;
- short silent motion loops;
- sponsor-neutral master assets.

Obtain model, location, team-mark, and media permissions before publication.

### Video behavior

- No sound by default.
- Provide a static poster image.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Avoid autoplay on constrained connections when it harms performance.
- Captions are required when speech conveys information.
- Do not make video the only way to understand the page.

## Layout system

### Grid

- Desktop: 12 columns
- Tablet: 8 columns
- Mobile: 4 columns
- Maximum content width: approximately 1,440 px
- Reading-width text: approximately 680–760 px
- Edge gutters: responsive, never less than 20 px on common mobile screens

### Spacing

Use an 8 px base system with purposeful exceptions for optical alignment.

Suggested section spacing:

- large editorial sections: 96–144 px desktop, 64–88 px mobile;
- standard sections: 72–96 px desktop, 48–72 px mobile;
- card padding: 24–40 px desktop, 20–28 px mobile;
- compact data rows: 12–20 px vertical.

Do not reduce vertical spacing merely to place more content above the fold. The first viewport needs clarity, not completeness.

### Shape and surface

- Prefer squared or slightly softened corners over heavily rounded consumer-app cards.
- Use lines, blocks, image crops, and contrast to create hierarchy.
- Reserve pills for statuses, filters, or truly compact metadata.
- Avoid placing every section inside a card.
- Use depth sparingly; strong flat contrast is preferred to heavy shadows.

## Interaction and motion

Motion should reinforce competition and state change without making the website feel like a game intro.

Recommended:

- short image and headline reveals;
- score/result state transitions;
- restrained hover crop or scale on media cards;
- clear nav and filter transitions;
- intentional page transition only if it does not delay navigation;
- motion durations generally within 150–400 ms.

Avoid:

- scroll hijacking;
- mandatory horizontal scroll for ordinary content;
- long entrance sequences;
- constantly moving background text;
- hover-only information;
- animation that obscures score, schedule, or status changes.

All meaningful interactions must work without motion and with keyboard input. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Responsive behavior

The website is mobile-first because captains, players, referees, and spectators will frequently use it near matchday.

### Mobile priorities

- season status and current primary action;
- next fixture or registration deadline;
- schedule and result scanning;
- standings position and points;
- venue directions and field information;
- form completion;
- urgent schedule/weather notice;
- contact/support route.

### Responsive rules

- Navigation collapses to a full-screen or contained mobile menu with visible primary action.
- The hero preserves message clarity before image spectacle.
- Tables use a deliberate mobile model; do not simply shrink desktop columns.
- Two-column editorial layouts stack in content-priority order.
- Large display type wraps intentionally and never obscures calls to action.
- Filters become a compact drawer or horizontally scrollable control only when accessible.
- Touch targets should be at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels.
- Sticky mobile actions may be used during registration but must not cover content or consent controls.

## SEO and discoverability

- Create unique titles and descriptions for every major page.
- Use one clear page heading and semantic heading order.
- Provide durable URLs for competitions, teams, fixtures, match results, stories, and policies.
- Add structured data appropriate to the actual organization, events, articles, and breadcrumbs when content exists.
- Generate XML sitemap and robots controls.
- Provide descriptive image alt text and social sharing images.
- Do not create thin placeholder pages solely to target keywords.
- Treat “Houston 9v9 soccer league,” “adult soccer league Houston,” and related terms as research topics, not permission for repetitive keyword copy.

## Accessibility requirements

Target WCAG 2.2 AA from the first design review.

Required practices:

- full keyboard access and visible focus;
- logical heading and landmark structure;
- sufficient text and interface contrast;
- accessible form labels, errors, instructions, and confirmation;
- no information communicated by color alone;
- alt text and decorative-image handling;
- captions/transcripts for meaningful media;
- reduced-motion support;
- scalable text and reflow at zoom;
- accessible tables with proper headers and captions;
- descriptive link and button labels;
- accessible urgent notices and schedule changes;
- documented accommodation contact.

Accessibility is part of acceptance criteria, not a post-launch enhancement.

## Performance requirements

- Target a Largest Contentful Paint of 2.5 seconds or better at the 75th percentile on public core pages.
- Reserve image dimensions to prevent layout shift.
- Use modern responsive image formats and sizes.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold media.
- Keep the first hero purposeful; do not ship multiple full-resolution images or a large video by default.
- Subset and preload only essential fonts.
- Minimize third-party scripts and review every marketing tag.
- Provide useful content if analytics, video, maps, or social embeds fail.
- Test on common mid-range mobile devices and constrained mobile connections.

## Website acceptance criteria

Release A is ready when:

- a visitor can explain what HTX Super League is after viewing the hero and first supporting section;
- proposed details are visibly distinguished from confirmed commitments;
- a qualified captain can submit interest on mobile without creating an account;
- a prospective partner can understand the opportunity and submit an inquiry;
- the HTX and TRIMNDS relationship is clear but HTX remains the lead identity;
- all navigation, forms, and meaningful content work with keyboard and screen-reader patterns;
- core pages meet the agreed responsive and performance targets;
- no empty competition pages, unsupported claims, unapproved partner marks, or misleading event imagery are published;
- form data has an approved owner, destination, retention rule, and response process;
- privacy, accessibility, and contact information are available;
- content editors can update current season status and calls to action without a code deployment.

## Recommended immediate design sequence

1. Approve the creative territory and provisional typography/color direction.
2. Produce two distinct Home-page visual explorations using the same content hierarchy.
3. Select the direction and build the responsive token/component foundation.
4. Design the complete Release A page set.
5. Design future Schedule, Standings, Team, and Match components so the public system can grow coherently.
6. Complete accessibility, content, and responsive review.
7. Build the pre-launch site and interest forms.
8. Replace provisional content and imagery as founder, venue, competition, and legal decisions are approved.

This sequence allows the website to create momentum now while protecting HTX Super League from presenting planning assumptions as binding league commitments.
