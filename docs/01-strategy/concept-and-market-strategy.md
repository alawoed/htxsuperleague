# Concept and Market Strategy

**Status:** Draft  
**Decision owner:** Founders  
**Research basis:** [Houston market scan](../research/houston-market-scan.md)

## Concise assessment

HTX Super League is a strong venture concept if it begins as a focused competition operator supported by software, rather than as a software platform searching for a league. Houston has visible small-sided demand, a large and diverse population, established venues, and a post-2026-World-Cup soccer environment. The market is also crowded and operationally unforgiving.

The opportunity is to combine five qualities that are often delivered unevenly:

1. dependable competition operations;
2. credible rules, officials, safety, and discipline;
3. professional presentation and community storytelling;
4. transparent digital administration;
5. measurable value for sponsors and venue partners.

The main risks are field availability and total venue cost, referee quality and behavior management, weather and player safety, weak team retention, unclear positioning, legal/insurance gaps, and building more software than the pilot needs.

## Recommended strategic position

### Category

A Houston competition and soccer-community brand, beginning with organized adult 9v9.

### Positioning statement

For Houston teams and players who want serious competition without semi-professional complexity, HTX Super League delivers a well-run 9v9 season with dependable operations, transparent rules, useful digital tools, and matchday storytelling.

### Brand promise

**Every match should feel organized, competitive, and worth showing up for.**

### What HTX Super League should not be

- a casual pickup organizer;
- a claim of semi-professional soccer without the standards to support it;
- a lowest-price league;
- a statistics app disconnected from real competition operations;
- a multi-city, multi-format marketplace at launch;
- a youth operator before safeguarding and child-participant requirements are intentionally designed.

## Initial customer

The recommended planning persona is an adult team captain who already organizes 12–16 players, has experienced inconsistent local leagues, and will pay for reliability if pricing and policies are transparent.

The founders must validate and select one initial segment:

| Segment | Advantages | Risks | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Men's open 18+ | Larger apparent team pool; straightforward roster rules | Discipline risk; crowded market | Recommended validation baseline |
| Women's open 18+ | Potentially underserved; distinctive community position | Demand concentration and scheduling need validation | Interview now; possible focused launch or second division |
| Coed 18+ | Social reach and mixed audience | Complex gender and roster rules; several existing offers | Test, but do not combine with men's/open in one pilot |
| Over-30 | Strong retention and behavior potential | Smaller pool and age verification | Viable alternative if interviews show better demand |
| Youth | Larger family ecosystem and sponsor appeal | Substantially greater safeguarding, consent, staffing, scheduling, and legal duties | Defer |

## Customer value by stakeholder

| Stakeholder | Primary job | HTX value proposition |
|---|---|---|
| Captain | Assemble and manage a competitive team | Clear registration, roster control, reliable schedule, fast support, transparent charges |
| Player | Compete, improve, and be recognized | Safe matches, credible officials, profile/stats, photos/highlights, awards |
| Referee | Work quality matches safely and get paid correctly | Complete assignments, rules, rosters, support, incident tools, predictable payment |
| Spectator | Follow people and teams they care about | Accurate schedule, venue details, scores, standings, stories, media |
| Sponsor | Reach an identifiable Houston soccer audience | Defined inventory, brand-safe events, measurable delivery and reporting |
| Venue | Monetize fields without operational disorder | Predictable bookings, insured use, responsible participants, one accountable operator |

## Differentiation pillars

### Reliability

Publish schedules on time, communicate changes through one source of truth, start matches promptly, and resolve questions within stated service levels.

### Competition integrity

Verify eligibility, lock rosters, use clear standings logic, protect officials, document discipline, and offer a bounded appeal process.

### Presentation

Create a consistent weekly destination with signage, team identity, photography, results graphics, awards, and stories.

### Transparency

Show what fees include, how weather and refunds work, how standings are calculated, and how decisions are made.

### Digital usefulness

Reduce captain and administrator work. Technology succeeds when it prevents confusion and manual errors, not when it adds features.

## Validation plan

### Discovery sample

- 25 current or recent team captains;
- 10–15 players who are not captains;
- 5–8 referees or assignors;
- 5 venue operators;
- 5 prospective sponsors or community partners.

Recruit across geography, gender, age, skill, price sensitivity, and existing league affiliation.

### Questions to answer

- What causes teams to leave or avoid a league?
- Which nights, areas, surfaces, and travel times are viable?
- What does “competitive” mean to the target team?
- What is the total amount teams currently pay, including weekly and roster charges?
- Which included services materially affect willingness to pay?
- How are disputes, forfeits, bad fields, weak officiating, and weather handled today?
- Would captains place a refundable deposit, and under what terms?
- Which media and statistics features are actually valued?
- What sponsor categories fit this audience without harming trust?

### Demand tests

1. Publish a focused landing page with one segment, tentative area, format, season window, price range, and clear non-binding status.
2. Measure qualified captain leads, not only email signups.
3. Invite qualified teams to an information session and venue preview.
4. Offer a small refundable reservation deposit only after refund terms and payment controls are ready.
5. Require eight paid team commitments and maintain a two-team qualified waitlist before the no-return venue commitment.

### Validation thresholds

Proceed to commitment only when:

- at least 12 qualified teams express segment- and schedule-specific interest;
- at least 8 indicate willingness at the tested total price;
- at least 3 venue options are operationally viable and 1 is financially viable;
- an officials path and insurance/sanctioning path are documented;
- sponsor conversations support the budget assumption or the founders approve the gap;
- the pilot cash exposure fits the founders' approved limit.

## Go-to-market approach

### Team acquisition

- founder and captain networks;
- direct outreach to teams in existing leagues during off-season windows;
- captain referral credit that does not distort competition;
- targeted social content featuring the actual venue and operating promise;
- open evaluation/showcase event only if it contributes to team formation;
- partnerships with training groups, soccer retailers, community organizations, and venue operators.

### Conversion assets

- concise competition overview;
- exact fee inclusions and payment schedule;
- venue photos, surface rules, parking, and travel information;
- rules summary and discipline promise;
- season calendar and rainout approach;
- sample matchday presentation;
- founder and TRIMNDS credibility;
- clear refund and launch-threshold terms.

### Retention loop

Capture captain feedback after weeks 1, 3, and 6; resolve operational friction during the season; publish visible improvements; open returning-team priority registration only after the pilot review.

## Strategic metrics

Track the funnel from qualified captain lead to team deposit to fully paid team to completed season to return intent. Do not use followers or total website traffic as primary evidence.

Core metrics:

- qualified team leads by source;
- deposit and full-payment conversion;
- effective acquisition cost per team;
- roster activation and waiver completion;
- match completion and on-time start rate;
- support response/resolution time;
- forfeits and disciplinary incidents per match;
- captain and player satisfaction;
- team retention intent and actual renewal;
- contribution margin per team and per field hour;
- sponsor revenue, delivery rate, and renewal intent.

## Strategic conclusion

Proceed with structured validation. Do not yet commit to a public launch date, fixed price, or custom application delivery date. The venture earns the right to build when demand, venue, officials, insurance, founder governance, and pilot economics align.
