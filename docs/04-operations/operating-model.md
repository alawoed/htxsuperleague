# Operating Model and Staffing

**Status:** Draft  
**Decision owner:** Venture lead / commissioner

## Operating principle

HTX Super League is an events and competition operation before it is a software company. The operating model must make every published match safe, staffed, officiated, recorded, communicated, and financially controlled.

## Pilot organization

One person may hold multiple roles, but accountabilities must remain explicit. Safety monitoring, financial approval, and disciplinary appeal should not be concentrated in one conflicted individual.

| Role | Core responsibilities | Pilot model |
|---|---|---|
| Venture lead / commissioner | Overall delivery, founder reporting, partner escalation, launch authority | Founder or appointed part-time operator |
| Competition manager | Rules, eligibility, fixtures, results, standings, discipline administration | Part-time; may be combined with commissioner |
| Operations and venue lead | Facility contract, field readiness, equipment, vendors, weather, matchday staff | Part-time with matchday presence |
| Registrar / customer support | Team onboarding, rosters, waivers, payments status, captain communication | Part-time; may be shared with competition manager |
| Referee assignor | Officials recruitment, assignment, replacement, quality feedback, payment approval | Qualified contractor or association partner |
| Medical/safety provider | Emergency readiness and participant care within contracted scope | Venue-required or contracted professional |
| Matchday manager | Check-in, schedule control, incident coordination, score/report collection | One accountable person per event day |
| Finance administrator | Budget, reconciliation, invoices, refunds, contractor records, reporting | TRIMNDS/shared or fractional |
| Brand and commercial lead | Marketing, sponsors, media, community, fulfillment | Founder/shared role |
| Photographer/content operator | Approved capture, file delivery, consent-aware publishing | Contractor or rotating approved team |
| Product owner | Workflow, system acceptance, release priorities, support feedback | Shared TRIMNDS capability |
| Technical lead | Security, implementation, integrations, reliability, incident response | Assigned only after build approval |

## Founder and management cadence

- Weekly founder operating review during validation and build.
- Weekly cash, registrations, venue, officials, risks, and launch-readiness report from registration opening.
- Event-day operations briefing before the first match.
- Incident review within 24 hours for material safety, conduct, financial, venue, or system events.
- Season scorecard at midpoint and close.

## Core operating processes

### Team acquisition and acceptance

1. Qualify the captain, team level, preferred geography, and availability.
2. Provide the offer, price, included services, rules summary, and refund terms.
3. Collect an application or conditional reservation.
4. Review team identity, conduct history where lawfully available, roster viability, and competitive fit.
5. Accept the team and collect payment under published terms.
6. Assign the captain onboarding checklist and deadlines.

Acceptance criteria must be objective. The league may decline a team for safety, prior serious misconduct, unpaid balances, false information, or competitive-fit reasons stated in policy, subject to legal review.

### Participant registration

1. Captain invites each player.
2. Player creates or verifies an account.
3. Player provides minimum eligibility data.
4. Player accepts waiver, code of conduct, privacy notice, and media choice.
5. Payment or team-covered status is confirmed.
6. Registrar approves eligibility.
7. Player is added to the active roster and becomes check-in eligible.

### Fixture management

- Build venue inventory first.
- Generate a fair draft schedule.
- Check team conflicts, slot distribution, rest, holidays, and venue constraints.
- Approve and publish one official version.
- Version every change and notify affected parties.
- Track cause, notice, cost, and outcome for every change.

### Match and result management

1. Inspect field, goals, weather, equipment, emergency access, and staff readiness.
2. Check in teams and officials.
3. Confirm match start, rosters, uniform colors, and match ball.
4. Monitor schedule and incidents without interfering with referee authority.
5. Collect referee match report and team confirmation.
6. Enter and validate score, cards, and approved events.
7. Publish result and updated standings.
8. Route incidents to the discipline process.

### Discipline

- Preserve the referee report and relevant evidence.
- Apply automatic suspensions immediately.
- Notify the charged person and captain of the allegation, interim status, response window, and process.
- Use the approved sanction matrix and document reasons.
- Keep competition administration separate from appeal review where practical.
- Communicate the outcome without publishing unnecessary personal information.

### Payments and refunds

- Use approved processor workflows and coded products/discounts.
- Reconcile transactions weekly.
- Require documented approval for manual adjustments.
- Apply the published refund policy consistently.
- Keep a refund and chargeback register with reason and decision owner.

## Matchday staffing baseline

For one active 9v9 field:

- one matchday manager;
- referee crew under the approved model;
- one registration/check-in and operations assistant during peak arrival;
- contracted medical/safety coverage as required;
- designated weather monitor who is not simultaneously refereeing or coaching;
- media operator when scheduled;
- venue or security personnel required by contract or risk assessment.

If multiple fields operate simultaneously, staffing must scale by field and line of sight. One person should not be assigned responsibilities that cannot be performed concurrently.

## Matchday timeline template

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| T-90 | Venue access, field/goal inspection, emergency access, weather review |
| T-60 | Staff and officials check-in; equipment, radios/phones, signage, sponsor assets |
| T-45 | First teams check in; rosters and waivers verified |
| T-15 | Captain/referee briefing; uniform and match-ball confirmation |
| T | Kickoff |
| Match end + 10 | Referee report and team result confirmation |
| Match end + 20 | Next teams ready; field and equipment reset |
| Event close | Venue sweep, incident confirmation, equipment count, sponsor proof capture |
| Close + 12 hours | Results and standings published target |
| Next business day | Payment, incident, customer, and vendor follow-up |

## Service standards

| Service | Proposed standard |
|---|---|
| General inquiry acknowledgement | Within 1 business day |
| Payment/roster issue acknowledgement | Same business day during launch week |
| Schedule publication | At least 14 days before opening match; target earlier |
| Planned schedule change | Notify immediately after approval through official channels |
| Weather decision | By stated decision time when foreseeable; real-time when conditions change suddenly |
| Result publication | 95% within 12 hours |
| Correctable stat dispute | Submit within 48 hours; resolve within 2 business days |
| Discipline notice | Within 2 business days, faster for next-match eligibility |
| Refund decision | Within 5 business days after complete information |

## Vendor and contractor controls

- Written scope, rate, cancellation, insurance, deliverables, confidentiality, IP, data, and payment terms.
- Background/safeguarding requirements where participant group or governing rules require them.
- W-9 and tax classification collected securely.
- Named substitute or replacement process for critical roles.
- Performance and incident feedback after each event.
- No contractor receives more participant data than their job requires.

## Operational dashboards

The commissioner dashboard should show:

- paid and pending teams;
- roster, waiver, and eligibility completion;
- venue slots and fixture status;
- referee and staff assignment coverage;
- open customer and discipline cases;
- upcoming weather decision points;
- cash collected, due, refunded, and committed;
- sponsor deliverables due;
- launch-readiness blockers;
- match completion, on-time starts, forfeits, and incidents.

## Operating readiness gate

The commissioner may authorize opening day only when venue access, field readiness, officials, medical/safety coverage, emergency plan, insurance, paid teams, eligible rosters, rules, communication systems, payment controls, and match reporting have all passed documented checks.
