# Pilot Season and Launch Roadmap

**Status:** Proposed  
**Decision owner:** Venture lead / commissioner  
**Planning date:** 2026-07-15  
**Recommended pilot:** Spring 2027, with exact dates set only after venue contracting

## Launch strategy

The first season is a controlled proof of operations. The league should launch one product, at one venue or tight venue cluster, for one customer segment, with enough time to resolve safety, legal, financial, and software dependencies.

## Phased plan

### Phase A: Founder alignment

**Proposed window:** July 15–31, 2026

Deliverables:

- approve or amend the venture charter;
- name the venture lead and functional owners;
- define voting, reserved matters, deadlock, and spending thresholds;
- approve the validation budget and maximum exploratory commitments;
- select the initial segment hypotheses;
- approve the founder decision register and weekly cadence.

Exit gate:

- ownership and authority are clear;
- no external party can receive an unauthorized commercial or launch promise.

### Phase B: Concept and demand validation

**Proposed window:** August 1–September 4, 2026

Deliverables:

- 25 captain interviews and 10+ player interviews;
- referee/assignor, venue, sponsor, and community interviews;
- structured competitor offer and total-cost comparison;
- segment, schedule, geography, price, and experience hypotheses;
- landing page and qualified captain interest list;
- initial brand/domain/trademark screening.

Exit gate:

- at least 12 qualified team leads for the same proposed segment and schedule;
- at least 8 demonstrate credible willingness at the tested effective price;
- no unresolved evidence that invalidates the positioning.

Stop or pivot if demand is fragmented across incompatible formats, nights, locations, or price points.

### Phase C: Operational and financial feasibility

**Proposed window:** September 8–October 16, 2026

Deliverables:

- at least 5 written venue quotes and site inspections;
- preferred and backup venue options with contract-risk review;
- officials model, availability, and quote;
- TSSAS/USASA versus independent insurance memo;
- medical/safety coverage recommendation;
- quote-based season budget, cash-flow forecast, and cancellation exposure;
- proposed competition format, rules outline, and calendar;
- operating entity and legal work plan;
- initial sponsor interest and cash assumptions.

Exit gate:

- one viable operating configuration fits the approved cash-loss ceiling;
- venue, official, medical, legal, insurance, and sponsor dependencies have owners and deadlines;
- founders approve the commit budget.

Do not sign a material non-refundable venue contract if the demand and funding gates are not met, unless founders explicitly approve the full exposure.

### Phase D: Offer, policy, brand, and product definition

**Proposed window:** October 19–December 11, 2026

Deliverables:

- approved customer segment, competition promise, dates, price, and inclusions;
- brand identity foundation and “A TRIMNDS Venture” usage;
- rules, waiver, code, privacy, refund, weather, discipline, appeal, and safety workstreams under review;
- signed or execution-ready venue/official/vendor terms;
- approved sponsor inventory and sales materials;
- approved P0 product requirements, roles, data inventory, architecture decisions, budget, and delivery owner;
- captain acquisition and communications plan.

Exit gate:

- the public offer is accurate, operationally deliverable, financially modeled, and legally reviewable;
- founders approve the software build or controlled third-party workflow.

### Phase E: Build, configure, sell, and test

**Proposed window:** November 16, 2026–January 29, 2027

Work may overlap Phase D only for reversible prototypes and vendor setup. Production implementation starts after build approval.

Deliverables:

- public site and captain interest/application experience;
- hosted payment and refund controls;
- player, roster, waiver, schedule, result, standings, discipline, and administration P0 workflows;
- staging environment and synthetic season simulation;
- sponsor agreements and asset fulfillment plan;
- team information sessions and conditional commitments;
- matchday operating manual and staff/vendor recruitment;
- monitoring, backups, security, accessibility, and outage fallback.

Exit gate:

- critical workflows pass end-to-end testing;
- support, matchday, finance, and incident owners can operate the system;
- legal text and policies are approved for registration use.

### Phase F: Registration and preseason readiness

**Proposed window:** January 4–February 19, 2027

Deliverables:

- 8 accepted and paid teams plus a 2-team qualified waitlist;
- complete rosters, policy acceptances, eligibility, and balances by deadline;
- published schedule and final rules;
- referee assignments and backup coverage;
- venue, medical, security, equipment, weather, and emergency checks;
- captain orientation and staff tabletop exercise;
- sponsor assets approved and installed/scheduled;
- launch readiness review with documented sign-off.

Exit gate:

- every opening-day P0 dependency is green or has an explicitly accepted contingency;
- commissioner authorizes launch.

### Phase G: Pilot season

**Proposed competition window:** February 20–April 24, 2027  
**Weather buffers and close:** Through May 2027

Operating cadence:

- matchday readiness and close checklist every week;
- weekly operations, cash, support, incident, and sponsor report;
- captain pulse after weeks 1, 3, and 6;
- midpoint founder review;
- no unapproved expansion or major feature work during the season;
- urgent safety, legal, security, and integrity fixes take priority.

### Phase H: Close and decision

**Proposed window:** Within 30 days of the final

Deliverables:

- final financial close and cash reconciliation;
- match, forfeit, delay, incident, discipline, and support review;
- captain, player, referee, sponsor, venue, and staff feedback;
- sponsor fulfillment reports;
- product performance, security, and data-retention actions;
- founder recommendation: repeat, improve, expand, pause, or stop;
- season-two decision records and budget.

## Launch readiness checklist

### Business and finance

- approved entity and authority;
- approved budget, cash reserve, bank/payment controls, and accounting process;
- 8 paid teams and waitlist threshold;
- signed venue, officials, medical, and critical vendor agreements;
- sponsor cash received according to contract;
- cancellation and worst-case cash exposure accepted.

### Competition and people

- approved rules, format, fixture fairness, and tiebreak tests;
- all player eligibility, rosters, and policy acceptances complete;
- officials assigned and replacement process tested;
- staff trained; conflicts and escalation contacts documented;
- discipline and appeal panel/authority ready.

### Safety and legal

- insurance active and certificates delivered;
- venue permissions and additional-insured requirements satisfied;
- EAP, AED, medical, weather, heat, lightning, and incident procedures approved;
- field and goal inspection responsibilities assigned;
- customer terms, privacy, waiver, refund, media, and code documents effective;
- secure incident and legal record locations ready.

### Product and communication

- production workflows tested with synthetic teams and all match states;
- roles/MFA/audit configured;
- payment webhooks and reconciliation tested;
- backup/restore and matchday outage fallback tested;
- schedule, venue, rules, help, and sponsor content published;
- urgent and routine communication templates approved.

## Pilot decision scorecard

At season close, score each category green, amber, or red:

| Category | Core question |
|---|---|
| Demand | Did eight teams pay, participate, and create a credible renewal base? |
| Experience | Did captains, players, and officials perceive the league as safe, fair, and organized? |
| Delivery | Were matches, schedules, results, standings, and cases handled to standard? |
| Economics | Did actual cost and contribution support repetition at a viable price and sponsor level? |
| Partnerships | Did venue and sponsors want to renew under workable terms? |
| Risk | Were incidents within the organization's competence and controls? |
| Product | Did the system reduce work and errors enough to justify continued investment? |
| Team | Can the operating roles be staffed sustainably without founder burnout? |

Expansion is allowed only when all critical categories are green and no red category is hidden by one-time founder effort.
